### COMPLETE STRATEGY REFERENCE LIBRARY

**TIER 1 - FOUNDATIONAL (Low-Medium Complexity):**

**1. Solo 401(k) / SEP IRA / Retirement Maximization**
- **Requirements**: Self-employment income (any amount)
- **Value Range**: $2,000-$25,500 in tax savings
- **Calculation**: 
  - Employee contribution: Up to $23,000 (2025)
  - Employer contribution: Up to 25% of net SE income
  - Total limit: $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+)
  - Savings = Total contribution √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Anyone with side business or self-employment income
- **Time to implement**: 2-4 hours initial, 1 hour annually
- **Difficulty**: Low-Medium

**2. Backdoor Roth IRA**
- **Requirements**: Income above Roth limits ($161K single, $240K married)
- **Value Range**: $1,500-$3,000 in long-term tax savings
- **Calculation**: 
  - Contribute $7,000 to Traditional IRA ($8,000 if 50+)
  - Convert to Roth immediately
  - Future growth is tax-free
  - Immediate savings = avoiding future taxes on growth
- **Best for**: High earners who can't contribute directly to Roth
- **Time to implement**: 1-2 hours
- **Difficulty**: Low

**3. S-Corporation Election**
- **Requirements**: $60,000+ net self-employment profit
- **Value Range**: $5,000-$20,000+ in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Save 15.3% SE tax on distributions above reasonable salary
  - Example: $150K profit, $70K salary = $80K distributions
  - Savings = $80K √ó 15.3% = $12,240
- **Best for**: Established businesses with consistent high profits
- **Time to implement**: 8-12 hours initial, 3-5 hours monthly
- **Difficulty**: Medium-High

**4. Home Office Deduction**
- **Requirements**: Dedicated home space for business use
- **Value Range**: $500-$5,000 in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Simplified: $5/sq ft (max 300 sq ft) = up to $1,500 deduction
  - Regular: (Office sq ft / Total sq ft) √ó Total home expenses
  - Savings = Deduction √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Anyone working from home regularly
- **Time to implement**: 2-3 hours
- **Difficulty**: Low

**5. Augusta Rule (Section 280A)**
- **Requirements**: Own home + have a business entity
- **Value Range**: $2,000-$14,000 in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Can rent home to business up to 14 days/year
  - Determine market rate (typically $350-$1,000/day)
  - Max deduction: 14 days √ó daily rate
  - Savings = Deduction √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Business owners who own their home
- **Time to implement**: 3-4 hours
- **Difficulty**: Low-Medium

**6. HSA Maximization**
- **Requirements**: High-deductible health plan (HDHP)
- **Value Range**: $1,200-$3,500 in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - 2025 limits: $4,150 (self) / $8,300 (family)
  - Plus $1,000 catch-up if 55+
  - Triple tax advantage: Deductible + grows tax-free + withdrawals tax-free
  - Savings = Contribution √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Anyone with HDHP
- **Time to implement**: 30 minutes
- **Difficulty**: Very Low

**7. W-4 Optimization**
- **Requirements**: W-2 employment
- **Value Range**: Improved cash flow of $200-$800/month
- **Calculation**:
  - Reduce overwithholding to minimize refund
  - Increase take-home pay throughout year
  - Value = better use of your own money vs. interest-free loan to IRS
- **Best for**: Anyone getting large tax refunds
- **Time to implement**: 30 minutes
- **Difficulty**: Very Low

**8. Accountable Plan**
- **Requirements**: Business with employees (can be spouse)
- **Value Range**: $2,000-$8,000 in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Reimburse employees for business expenses
  - Typical reimbursements: $3,000-$10,000/employee
  - Includes: Mileage, cell phone, internet, home office, supplies
  - Savings = Total reimbursements √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Business owners with spouse or family employees
- **Time to implement**: 3-4 hours
- **Difficulty**: Low-Medium

**9. Vehicle Expense Optimization**
- **Requirements**: Business vehicle use >50%
- **Value Range**: $2,000-$15,000 in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Standard mileage: 70¬¢/mile (2025) √ó business miles
  - Actual expense: Total costs √ó business use %
  - Section 179/Bonus: Up to 100% depreciation year 1 for heavy vehicles
  - Savings = Deduction √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Business owners with significant vehicle use
- **Time to implement**: 2-3 hours initial, 15 min/month tracking
- **Difficulty**: Low-Medium

**TIER 2 - REAL ESTATE (Medium-High Complexity):**

**10. Cost Segregation Study**
- **Requirements**: Own investment/business property worth $500K+
- **Value Range**: $15,000-$150,000+ in accelerated tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Reclassify 20-40% of building cost to 5/7/15-year property
  - Accelerate depreciation vs. 27.5/39-year standard
  - 100% bonus depreciation NOW PERMANENT (OBBBA 2025)
  - Example: $1M property ‚Üí $300K accelerated = $100K+ tax savings
- **Best for**: Real estate investors with recent property purchases
- **Time to implement**: 4-6 weeks (outsourced to engineer)
- **Difficulty**: Medium

**11. Short-Term Rental Loophole**
- **Requirements**: Own/manage STR with average stay <7 days + material participation
- **Value Range**: $5,000-$50,000+ in tax savings (varies widely)
- **Calculation**:
  - Unlike long-term rentals, losses can offset W-2/business income
  - Must materially participate (100+ hours, more than anyone else)
  - Losses from depreciation, expenses offset active income
  - Savings = Loss amount √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: W-2 earners with STR properties who can meet participation test
- **Time to implement**: Ongoing (need to meet hours requirement)
- **Difficulty**: Medium-High

**12. Real Estate Professional Status (REPS)**
- **Requirements**: 750+ hours/year in real estate activities + more than any other work
- **Value Range**: $10,000-$100,000+ in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - All rental losses become non-passive
  - Can offset W-2 and other active income
  - Unlimited loss deductions (not subject to $25K passive loss limit)
  - Savings = Total losses √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Spouse available full-time for RE, or dedicated RE investors
- **Time to implement**: Ongoing (year-long commitment)
- **Difficulty**: High

**TIER 3 - ADVANCED BUSINESS (High Complexity):**

**13. QBI Deduction (Section 199A)**
- **Requirements**: Pass-through business income (not W-2)
- **Value Range**: $3,000-$30,000+ in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - 20% deduction on qualified business income
  - Phase-outs for service businesses above $191K (single)/$383K (married)
  - Made PERMANENT by OBBBA 2025
  - Savings = (QBI √ó 20%) √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: Business owners and independent contractors
- **Time to implement**: Automatic if eligible (CPA calculates)
- **Difficulty**: Low (to claim) / Medium (to optimize)

**14. Multiple Entity Structure**
- **Requirements**: Multiple income streams or business lines
- **Value Range**: $5,000-$30,000+ in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Varies: Can optimize QBI, reduce SE tax, protect assets
  - Example structures: Management company + operating entities
  - Can employ family members to shift income
  - Savings = Strategy-specific
- **Best for**: Sophisticated business owners with multiple ventures
- **Time to implement**: 10-20 hours
- **Difficulty**: High

**15. Cost Segregation for Business Property**
- **Requirements**: Own business property (office, warehouse, etc.)
- **Value Range**: $10,000-$100,000+ in accelerated savings
- **Calculation**: Same as residential cost seg
- **Best for**: Business owners who own their business real estate
- **Time to implement**: 4-6 weeks
- **Difficulty**: Medium

**16. Defined Benefit Plan**
- **Requirements**: High income ($300K+) + willing to commit to contributions
- **Value Range**: $30,000-$100,000+ per year in tax savings
- **Calculation**:
  - Can contribute $100K-$300K+/year depending on age
  - Much higher limits than 401(k)
  - Requires actuarial certification
  - Savings = Contribution √ó marginal rate
- **Best for**: High earners 50+ who want to supercharge retirement savings
- **Time to implement**: 8-12 hours initial setup
- **Difficulty**: High
